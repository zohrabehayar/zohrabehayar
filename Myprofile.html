Chief Information Officer (CIO)


My goal in my career is to be in senior management in the healthcare industry where you can have a real impact on the morale, leadership and systems that are in place in the organisation. The Chief Information Officer (CIO) is responsible for all the information technology in an organisation, they are also responsible for the strategy and implantation of new systems. They oversee the infrastructure of the information technology which is used in an organisation. I am a manager; I have management experience and I know the responsibility that you must carry when you are responsible for a team. I always tell people that the title might sound amazing, or the pay is higher, but it is not for everyone, it can be a thankless job and majority of the time you are a fixing a problem that no one else wants to fix. Even though management can be hard, mentally crushes you sometimes or you feel like you have taken 1 step forward and the next day you have taken 10 steps back, it is also highly rewarding. When you train, spend time with someone in your team who flourishes or the positive impact of the processes you have implemented has in the organisation that you work in; the feeling of success is euphoria.
